There is not a single moment I can hone in to, where the outcome significantly changed my life. However, I can show you a recurring situation which is pushing my life in a definite direction. It only occurs outside of school in the late spring, summer, and early fall seasons; and rarely. The colors that represent this:

Light Green, Really Light Blue, Brown, and Grays:

As the world creates the setting around me, the colors outside change to a unique palette.
The trunks darken, leaves burst, and trees weep from the weight of the sky.
The sky itself diffuses the sun, illuminating the entire sky evenly, hiding the shadows, and letting everthing shine.
Occasionally, with the sky being so heavy, it drowns out the sounds of nearby cars.

Vibrant Blue, Vibrant Red, and Vibrant Green:

The colors outside inspire me,
I surge with energy, passion, and ideas.
Every color a computer can make starts with three colors: red, green, and blue.
So my ideas are exactly that, mixtures of red, green, and blue, mixing into anything possible.

Orange and Dark Grey:

Orange and Dark Grey are the colors that help me suspend reality, keep focused. They push my work out of my screen so I can manipulate it.

Purple, Red, and Light Blue:

These are the colors of my emotions defining how I work. The purple is a sense of nervousness, unsettlement, pushing me to work. The red is my energy towards my work, but also the anger that I cannot complete it all. And light blue is the sadness and realization that I do not have enough time for all of these great ideas.
